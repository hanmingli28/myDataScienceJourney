Description

The transaction data, which is obtained via Twitter API, contains tweets on the topic of wildfire. The original text data is cleaned and transformed to the transaction data shown. Each row of the dataset represents one original tweet, with no duplicate words.

The top rules for support shows that {join} -> {wildfire} has the highest score. Since support measures the frequency of a itemset in a dataset, it indicates that these two words appear together the most often. The top rules for confidence all have confidence score = 1 because the threshold of confidence is set to be 1. Confidence is an indication of how often the rule has been found to be true. Lift is an indication of if the itemsets involved in the rule are associated with each other or independent. It also measures how strongly itemsets are associated. The 15 top rules for Lift shows that {severe} is heavily associated with {seasons} with a Lift score of 21.

Discussion

The three network visualizations showcased above indicate that the itemset {severe} is heavily associated with {seasons}. This phenomenon is easy to understand as people tend to discuss the severity of the wildfire with respect to the season where the fire took place.

In addition, the high occurence of itemsets such as {severe}, {devastating}, {stop} shows that the severity of the wildire,the damage caused by the fire and how to stop/contain the fire are one of the most common things people tweet about when comes to wildfire. Also, the high occurence of {severe} is an indication of the devastating power of wildfire since the itemset is almost ubiquitous in the network.
